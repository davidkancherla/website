Two sides, one feed.

For consumers

Find the deal you didn't know was happening 200 feet away.

Every restaurant, gym, salon, and shop running a real promotion right now — surfaced by location, time, and what you actually care about. No hunting through Instagram. No expired Groupon coupons.

  • Live, time-bound deals — not stale offers
  • Hyperlocal: walking-distance default, custom radius optional
  • Filter by category, price, end-time, dietary needs
  • One tap to claim — no QR scavenger hunts
For merchants

Fill empty seats and clear inventory in minutes.

Got 12 unsold reservations at 7pm? A quiet Tuesday afternoon? Inventory that won't survive the week? Push a deal and reach the people standing within walking distance — right now. No ad spend, no contracts.

  • Self-serve — list a deal in under 60 seconds
  • Pay only when a customer redeems
  • Time-bound deals built in (no manual cleanup)
  • Insights on who's nearby and what's converting
02

How it works

i.
Merchants post live deals
A coffee shop kicks off a 90-min flash deal. A gym lists "first month free, today only." A salon opens a 4pm walk-in slot at 40% off. All from a phone, in seconds.
ii.
Consumers see what's nearby, now
The DealRadar app surfaces deals within a configurable radius, filtered by category and time. Live countdowns. Real walking distances. AI-personalized ordering of what you'll actually use.
iii.
One-tap redemption
Consumer taps "Claim," shows the screen at checkout, deal applied. Merchant sees the redemption in their dashboard. No QR codes, no friction, no third-party booking layer.
03

Why now

i.
Local discovery is broken
Yelp is reviews, not deals. Instagram hides everything in stories. Groupon ages like milk. There's no live, location-aware, "what's happening RIGHT NOW" layer for local commerce.
ii.
Margins are tight, seats are empty
SMBs lose more revenue to empty Tuesdays than they ever recover from a busy Friday. They need a way to convert spare capacity into customers — without a $5k/month marketing contract.
iii.
AI makes ranking solvable
The hardest part of a two-sided local marketplace is showing the right deal to the right person at the right moment. Modern LLMs and embeddings make that tractable for the first time.
